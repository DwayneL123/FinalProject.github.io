Documentation Photo Gallery

This is the documentation of our journey on creating this website

First Meeting (April 23)

This photo was taken during our very first meeting, the moment everything started. We were still figuring things out, tossing around different ideas on what kind of website we should make. Everyone had something to share, and even though we were a bit unsure at first, it was fun seeing how our thoughts slowly came together. Some of us were writing things down, others were checking references on our phones, and a few were just throwing out ideas.

Research Planning (April 23)

After settling on a topic, our next step was figuring out how we’d divide the research and tasks that’s what this screenshot shows. We listed down all the important points we wanted to cover, from famous inventors to big breakthroughs in transportation and communication. It was a bit overwhelming at first, but seeing it organized like this helped everything feel more doable. Each of us picked sections we were most interested in, and just like that, we turned a huge task into something we could tackle together. It felt like we were finally getting somewhere.

First Coding Session (April 25)

This was during one of our Discord calls where we were figuring out how the homepage should look and actually started coding it live. A couple of us were screen sharing so everyone could chime in with ideas or help fix things on the spot. We were bouncing ideas off each other, tweaking the design, and seeing the site slowly come together.

Exchanging of Code (April 28)

For our website project, each member for the coding task was assigned a specific task or section to work on. Once someone finished their part, they would send the completed code through Discord so the rest of the team could review or integrate it into the main project. This helped us stay organized and made it easy to track progress. By sharing our updates in real time, we ensured that everyone stayed on the same page and that all the pieces of the website came together smoothly.

Second Coding Session (May 1)

During our second coding session, we hopped on another Discord call to continue building the website. Everyone stayed in sync while working on their assigned parts, just like before. We shared updates, gave each other quick feedback, and helped troubleshoot bugs as they came up. Even though we had our own individual tasks, being on call made it feel like we were coding side by side, it kept things efficient and collaborative.

Last Meeting (May 16)

On our final Discord call, everyone showed up. We spent the time checking in with each other and going through the last few things that needed to get done. It wasn’t just about finishing tasks, We really wanted to make sure nothing was missed. People gave quick updates, asked questions, and helped out where needed. By the end of the call, we were all on the same page and felt good about where things stood. It was a chill but super productive way to wrap everything up together.
