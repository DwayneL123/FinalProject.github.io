Thomas Edison

Thomas Edison was a prolific American inventor who held 1,093 patents and revolutionized the modern world with inventions like the phonograph, the incandescent light bulb, and motion-picture devices. Born in 1847 in Ohio, Edison had little formal schooling and was mostly self-taught. His early career as a telegrapher led him into the field of electrical innovation. In 1876, he established the world’s first industrial research lab in Menlo Park, New Jersey. Edison’s breakthroughs in electric power, sound recording, and communication technologies laid the foundation for the modern technological age. Despite partial deafness, he became a symbol of American ingenuity and industrial progress.
