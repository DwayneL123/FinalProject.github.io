Karl Benz

Karl Benz was a German mechanical engineer who invented the first practical automobile powered by an internal-combustion engine in 1885. His three-wheeled Motorwagen marked the beginning of the modern automotive era. Benz & Co., founded in 1883, later developed four-wheeled cars and early racing vehicles. In 1926, his company merged with Daimler to form Daimler-Benz, creator of the Mercedes-Benz brand. Benz’s innovation laid the foundation for personal transportation and revolutionized global mobility, making him a central figure in automotive history.
