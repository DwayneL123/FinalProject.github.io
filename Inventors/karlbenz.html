The Internet is a vast, intricate network of interconnected computers and smart devices that share information through various telecommunications media. As a vital tool for business and information, it utilizes the TCP/IP protocol suite to facilitate a range of services, including web page access via HTTP, file transfer via FTP, email via POP3, SMTP, and IMAP4, and data transmission for wireless devices via WAP. However, the Internet in its current form is not inherently intelligent; it primarily serves as a publishing medium, disseminating data in the form of images and text for public or personal consumption. Just as individual magazines lack the ability to interact with each other's content without specific design, typical websites also cannot interact with one another unless intentionally programmed to do so. The World Wide Web is a complex web of information that relies on hyperlinks to enable browsers to navigate between sites, often in a non-linear fashion. Ultimately, the meaning, context, and relevance of web content require human interpretation.
