Thomas Edison

Thomas Edison was an American inventor and businessman who developed many important devices. Known as the "Wizard of Menlo Park", he was one of the first inventors to apply the principles of organized science and teamwork to the process of invention. His development of a practical incandescent light bulb, phonograph, and motion picture camera, along with his creation of the first industrial research laboratory, earned him the nickname "The Wizard of Menlo Park" and established him as one of history's most influential inventors.
