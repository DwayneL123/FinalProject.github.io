Hedy Lamarr

Hedy Lamarr was an Austrian-American actress famous for her glamorous roles in 1930s and 1940s Hollywood films. Beyond her film career, she was an inventive mind who, during World War II, co-developed a radio frequency-hopping technology designed to prevent signal jamming. Though not used in the war, this invention became foundational for modern wireless communication such as Wi-Fi and Bluetooth. Lamarr’s unique blend of beauty and brilliance has since earned her recognition as a pioneering figure in both entertainment and technology.
