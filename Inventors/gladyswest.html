Gladys West

Gladys West is an American mathematician whose pioneering work was essential to the development of the Global Positioning System (GPS). Born in rural Virginia in 1930, she excelled academically despite limited opportunities and became valedictorian of her high school. West earned degrees in mathematics and public administration and was hired by the U.S. Naval Proving Ground in 1956, where she used advanced mathematics and early computer programming to model Earth’s shape precisely. Her work on satellites like Seasat and GEOSAT laid the groundwork for accurate GPS technology. Recognized later in life for her vital contributions, West is celebrated as one of the “hidden figures” of science, breaking barriers for Black women in STEM.
