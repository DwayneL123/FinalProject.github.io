Martin Cooper

Martin Cooper is an American engineer widely regarded as the “father of the cellular phone.” In the early 1970s, he led the team at Motorola that developed the first truly portable mobile phone, the DynaTAC, and made the first public cell phone call in 1973. Cooper’s work revolutionized wireless communication by creating a phone that was not tied to a car, paving the way for modern mobile telephony. After leaving Motorola, he founded several companies advancing wireless technology. His contributions earned him the Charles Stark Draper Prize in 2013.
