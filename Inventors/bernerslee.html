Tim Berners-Lee

Tim Berners-Lee is a British computer scientist best known as the inventor of the World Wide Web. In 1989, while working at CERN, he proposed a system for sharing information using hypertext over the Internet, leading to the creation of the first web browser and server. His innovation transformed how people access and share information globally. In 1994, he founded the World Wide Web Consortium (W3C) to guide web standards. Knighted in 2004 and honored with the Millennium Technology Prize, Berners-Lee’s invention remains one of the most influential technological breakthroughs in history.
