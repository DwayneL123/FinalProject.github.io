George Washington Carver

George Washington Carver was an American agricultural scientist and inventor who transformed Southern farming after the Civil War. Born into slavery, he became a pioneering researcher at Tuskegee Institute, where he developed hundreds of products from peanuts, sweet potatoes, and soybeans to help farmers diversify crops and restore soil health. His innovations helped revive depleted Southern soils and improve the livelihoods of Black farmers. Carver’s dedication to education and sustainable agriculture made him a key figure in advancing farming practices and economic opportunities in the South.
