Nikola Tesla

Nikola Tesla was a Serbian-American inventor, engineer, and futurist best known for developing the alternating current (AC) system, which revolutionized the transmission of electricity. Born in 1856 in the Austrian Empire (now Croatia), he held over 300 patents and invented key technologies like the Tesla coil, induction motor, and remote control. After immigrating to the U.S. in 1884, he worked briefly with Thomas Edison before partnering with George Westinghouse to promote AC power. Notable Innovations

Alternating Current (AC) System

Year: 1887

Developed a complete system for generating and transmitting alternating current electricity, which became the standard for electrical power distribution.

Alternating Current (AC) System

Tesla Coil

Year: 1891

Invented a high-voltage, high-frequency transformer that enabled wireless transmission of electricity and is still used in radio technology and medical devices.

Tesla Coil

Induction Motor

Year: 1888

Designed a motor powered by alternating current, which became a cornerstone of modern electric motor technology.

Induction Motor

Radio

Year: 1893

Demonstrated wireless radio communication, laying the groundwork for modern wireless technology, though Marconi received the patent first.

Radio

Remote Control

Year: 1898

Invented and demonstrated a radio-controlled boat, pioneering the concept of wireless remote control.

Remote Control

Wardenclyffe Tower

Year: 1901

Began construction of a massive wireless transmission tower intended to provide free global wireless communication and power, though it was never completed.

Wardenclyffe Tower
