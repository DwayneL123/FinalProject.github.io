Charles Babbage

Charles Babbage was an English mathematician and inventor known as the “father of the computer” for conceiving the first automatic digital computer. In the 1820s, he designed the Difference Engine, a mechanical calculator capable of computing mathematical tables. Later, in the 1830s, he conceptualized the Analytical Engine, which included key features of modern computers: memory, control flow, and input via punched cards. Though never completed in his lifetime, Babbage’s ideas laid the groundwork for modern computing. He also contributed to statistics, postal reform, and invented devices like a speedometer and a locomotive cow catcher.
