Apollo 11 (Moon Landing)

Apollo 11 Moon Landing, 1969
Apollo Command and Service Module
Astronaut Buzz Aldrin in the Lunar Surface

Short Description

Apollo 11 was the first space flight in which humans walked on the moon. On July 20, 1969, Neil Armstrong and Buzz Aldrin stepped onto the lunar surface. The mission marked an important victory for the United States in the space race with the Soviet Union.

Purpose

Their goal was to complete a national challenge set by President Kennedy: to send a man to the moon and return him safely to Earth. In addition, scientific data should be collected and lunar exploration technologies tested.

How It Is Made

Apollo 11 was launched on a 111-meter-long Saturn V rocket and consisted of the command module Columbia, the service module and the lunar module Eagle. Together, they achieved space travel, landing on the moon and returning safely to Earth.

When It Is Made

Apollo 11 was launched on July 16, 1969, and returned on July 24 after eight days in space. The moon landing itself took place on July 20 at 4:17 p.m. Eastern Time.

It proved that human space exploration was possible and inspired generations in science and technology. Apollo 11 transformed how humanity sees itself that is capable of reaching beyond Earth into the universe.
