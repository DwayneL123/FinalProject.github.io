Short Description

The Hubble Space Telescope (HST) is a giant reflecting telescope in Earth's orbit that provides unparalleled clarity for seeing celestial objects. NASA launched it in 1990 to give high-resolution photographs of stars, galaxies, and other cosmic phenomena that are free of Earth's atmospheric imperfections.

Purpose

Hubble's primary mission is to study distant galaxies, nebulae, and other celestial objects in order to better understand how the universe formed and evolved. It helps researchers understand the universe's expansion pace, the life cycle of stars, and even the finding of exoplanets. Hubble has transformed our understanding of space with its detailed imaging and spectroscopic data.

How It Is Made

The Hubble Space Telescope was built by NASA and launched into orbit by the Space Shuttle Discovery. It comprises of a 2.4-meter primary mirror, multiple cameras, and spectrographs that catch visible, ultraviolet, and infrared light. The telescope was built to produce high-resolution photographs, and its optical system was later repaired after a problem with its mirror was discovered in 1993.

When It Is Made

The Hubble Space Telescope was launched on April 25, 1990, and has been operational for more than three decades. Despite initial problems, including an optical imperfection, it was rectified in 1993 and has remained one of the most important tools in space exploration.

Who Made It

NASA created Hubble with substantial assistance from aerospace businesses, scientists, and engineers. The telescope is named after the American astronomer Edwin Hubble, whose findings in the early twentieth century provided the framework for understanding the expanding universe.

Contribution To Society

The Hubble Space Telescope has revealed groundbreaking information about the universe's age, rate of expansion, and the origin of galaxies and stars. Its photographs and data have helped to locate new moons near Pluto, study the deep field of galaxies, and even identify the most distant galaxies.
