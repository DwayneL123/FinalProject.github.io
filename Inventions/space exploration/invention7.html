Short Description

NASA launched the Voyager probes in 1977 to investigate the outer planets like Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, and Neptune before eventually traveling into interstellar space.

Purpose

To collect and communicate data about the outer planets and their moons, rings, magnetic fields, and atmospheres, as well as to investigate the outer limit of the Sun's influence and continue to transmit information from interstellar space.

How It Is Made

Each Voyager spacecraft was outfitted with a high-gain antenna, ten scientific instruments (including cameras, magnetometers, and plasma detectors), power from radioisotope thermoelectric generators (RTGs), and onboard computers. They also brought the Golden Record, a gold-plated copper disc containing Earth sounds, images, and messages, in case there was extraterrestrial life.

When It Is Made

Voyager 2 launched on August 20, 1977, while Voyager 1 followed on September 5, 1977. They took advantage of a rare planetary alignment that allowed gravity to let them reach all four big outer worlds.

Who Made It

NASA's Jet Propulsion Laboratory (JPL), which is part of the California Institute of Technology, developed and operates it.

Contribution To Society

The Voyager spacecraft transformed our understanding of the outer solar system. They discovered active volcanoes on Io, studied Saturn's rings, and found new moons and magnetic fields on Uranus and Neptune. Voyager 1 was the first human-made object to enter interstellar space in 2012, and Voyager 2 followed in 2018. Their Golden Records represent humanity's yearning to communicate across the universe and have inspired generations of scientists, engineers, and space explorers.
