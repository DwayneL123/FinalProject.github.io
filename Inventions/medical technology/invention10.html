Short Description

In the modern era, a disease named COVID-19 began spreading in late 2019, eventually becoming a global pandemic. The virus, originally called SARS-CoV-2, emerged from Wuhan, China. COVID-19 led to widespread illness, death, and major disruptions globally. This virus is also known as severe acute respiratory syndrome coronavirus 2. The development of mRNA technology played a pivotal role in creating vaccines to combat the virus, with significant advancements made by scientists in this field.

Purpose

The primary purpose of the COVID-19 vaccine was to prevent the spread of the virus, reduce the severity of illness, and ultimately protect individuals from infection and death caused by COVID-19. Vaccines were developed quickly to curb the devastating global pandemic, saving lives and alleviating pressure on healthcare systems. The rapid development of mRNA vaccines like Pfizer-BioNTech and Moderna has also paved the way for future vaccine technologies, offering new tools to fight infectious diseases.

How It Is Made

The mRNA vaccines work by using messenger RNA (mRNA), a genetic material that instructs cells in the body to produce proteins similar to those found on the surface of the SARS-CoV-2 virus. These proteins then trigger an immune response, preparing the body to recognize and fight off the actual virus if it enters the system. The mRNA is synthetically created in a lab, which is then encapsulated in lipid nanoparticles for delivery into the cells. The vaccine does not alter a person's DNA, but instead stimulates the immune system to produce antibodies against the virus.

When It Is Made

The first known case of COVID-19 was found in Wuhan, China, in late 2019. The rapid spread of the virus across the globe led the World Health Organization to declare a pandemic on March 11, 2020. In response to the pandemic, the development of mRNA vaccines accelerated, with the Pfizer-BioNTech COVID-19 vaccine receiving full approval from the FDA in August 2021. Moderna’s vaccine was authorized for emergency use by the FDA shortly before that.

Who Made It

Two scientists, Katalin Karikó and Drew Weissman, played a critical role in making mRNA technology safe, effective, and usable for vaccines. Their groundbreaking work in modifying mRNA allowed it to evade the body’s immune defenses, making it possible to use mRNA in vaccines. Their research directly contributed to the development of mRNA vaccines for COVID-19, including the vaccines from Pfizer-BioNTech and Moderna.

Contribution To Society

The development of mRNA vaccines against COVID-19 has had a transformative impact on global health. These vaccines have helped to significantly reduce the number of COVID-19 cases, hospitalizations, and deaths worldwide. The mRNA technology used in these vaccines offers a quicker and more efficient way to develop vaccines, with the potential to address future pandemics more swiftly. Beyond COVID-19, this technology is being explored for use in vaccines for other diseases, including cancer and HIV, marking a major step forward in medical science. The approval and success of the COVID-19 vaccines demonstrated the power of scientific innovation and collaboration in combating global health crises.
