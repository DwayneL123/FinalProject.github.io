Minimally Invasive Surgery (MIS)

Minimally Invasive Surgery Procedure

Short Description

Minimally Invasive Surgery (MIS) is a medical technique that uses small incisions, specialized tools, and cameras to perform operations inside the body with less damage to surrounding tissues compared to traditional open surgery.

Purpose

The purpose of MIS is to reduce pain, scarring, and recovery time for patients. It aims to achieve the same results as open surgery but with fewer complications, shorter hospital stays, and quicker returns to normal activities.

How It Is Made

Minimally invasive procedures are done using tools such as laparoscopes (thin tubes with cameras), robotic arms, and tiny surgical instruments. Surgeons insert these through small cuts in the body and view the internal area on a monitor to guide the surgery with precision.

When It Is Made

MIS techniques began developing in the early 20th century, but modern laparoscopic surgery became widespread in the 1980s. The first successful laparoscopic gallbladder removal was performed in 1987, marking a key moment in MIS history.

Who Made It

Dr. Kurt Semm, a German gynecologist, was one of the pioneers of modern laparoscopic surgery. He performed the first laparoscopic appendectomy in 1983. His innovations helped expand the use of MIS across multiple fields of surgery.

Contribution To Society

Minimally Invasive Surgery has greatly improved patient outcomes by reducing surgical trauma, lowering infection risks, and speeding up recovery. It has transformed the field of surgery, making procedures safer, more efficient, and more comfortable for patients worldwide.
