Coal Power

Coal-fired power generation facility
Coal-fired power plants
Loy Yang Power Station, Australia

Short Description

Coal-fired power plants generate electricity by burning coal to produce high-pressure steam, which drives turbines linked to generators. Coal is abundant and cost-effective, making it a major global energy source.

Purpose

Coal power plants primarily provide affordable, reliable electricity to meet residential, commercial, and industrial demands. Coal is also used in steelmaking, cement production, and for by-products like activated carbon and carbon fiber.

How It Is Made

Coal (bituminous, subbituminous, or lignite) is burned in a boiler to heat water and convert it into high-pressure steam. This steam is then directed to spin the blades of a turbine, which is connected to a generator. The generator converts the mechanical energy of the spinning turbine into electrical energy.

When It Is Made

Coal has powered electricity generation since the Industrial Revolution, starting notably in Great Britain. Thomas Edison’s Holborn Viaduct station in London, opened in 1882, was the first public coal-fired power plant. Despite some regions phasing it out, coal remains a major global energy source.

Who Made It

The development of coal power involved many engineers and scientists during the Industrial Revolution. Thomas Edison’s establishment of the first public power plant was a key milestone.

Contribution To Society

Coal-fired power plants have been a vital source of affordable, reliable electricity, driving industrial growth and improving living standards. They help alleviate energy poverty and support industries like steel, cement, and manufacturing. However, the sector faces challenges in cutting carbon emissions to address climate change.
