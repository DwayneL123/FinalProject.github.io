Short Description

Solar panels, or photovoltaic (PV) panels, convert sunlight into electricity using energy from photons. This electricity powers everything from small devices to large residential and commercial systems.

Purpose

The main purpose of solar panels is to convert sunlight into clean, renewable electricity. They power off-grid systems, support telecommunications and remote sensing, and reduce dependence on conventional energy sources in homes and businesses.

How It Is Made

Solar panels are made up of many solar cells, usually crafted from silicon and layered with elements like phosphorus and boron to create an electric field. When sunlight hits the cells, it energizes electrons, knocking them loose and creating an electric current through the photovoltaic effect. These cells are connected to form panels, which can be grouped into larger arrays.

When It Is Made

Solar panel technology began with Edmond Becquerel’s discovery of the photovoltaic effect in the early 1800s. In 1893, Charles Fritts created the first true solar cell using selenium and gold. These early innovations laid the groundwork for the modern solar panels we use today.

Who Made It

The development of solar panels involved many pioneers. Edmond Becquerel’s discovery of the photovoltaic effect and Charles Fritts’s creation of the first solar cell were key milestones. Continued research and innovation over decades have led to the efficient solar technology we rely on today.

Contribution To Society

Solar panels generate clean, renewable electricity, helping reduce reliance on fossil fuels and lowering greenhouse gas emissions. They are ideal for off-grid use, supplying power to remote areas without costly infrastructure. As solar technology advances, it offers greater energy independence, long-term savings, and is becoming a key part of the global energy solution.
