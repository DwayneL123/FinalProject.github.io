Fire

Short Description

Fire is one of humanityâ€™s oldest technologies, with evidence suggesting its use began nearly two million years ago. Early use likely involved opportunistic harvesting from wildfires before humans learned to maintain and create fire.

Purpose

Early fire provided warmth and light, extending activity into the night and enabling migration to colder climates. It aided in food procurement through vegetation clearing, land fertilization, plant promotion, and animal trapping. Fire also served a social purpose, creating gathering points that fostered communication and social bonds.

How It Is Made

Sparks can be generated by striking certain stones like flint, quartz, and basalt. These sparks can then ignite readily flammable materials such as dry grass, moss, plant fibers, hair, and feathers.

When It Is Made

The controlled use of fire likely originated with Homo erectus during the Early Stone Age (Lower Paleolithic). The earliest archaeological evidence associated with humans comes from Oldowan hominid sites in the Lake Turkana region of Kenya. However, regular human-fire interactions likely predated the development of fixed hearths.

Who Made It

The controlled use of fire is attributed to our ancestor Homo erectus.

Contribution To Society

Fire fundamentally changed early human life. It provided essential warmth and light, revolutionized hunting and land management, and fostered social cohesion through communal gatherings. The development of pyrotechnology marked a significant step in human adaptation and cultural evolution..
