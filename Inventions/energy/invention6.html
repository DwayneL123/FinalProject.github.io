Short Description

Nuclear energy is released from atomic nuclei during nuclear reactions, mainly through controlled fission in reactors, where heavy atoms are split to produce large amounts of energy. While fusion offers future potential, it is still under development.

Purpose

The main purpose of nuclear power is to generate large, reliable amounts of electricity for homes, schools, workplaces, and hospitals, while producing minimal greenhouse gas emissions during operation.

How It Is Made

In nuclear power plants, uranium fuel (usually U-235) undergoes controlled fission, where its atoms split when struck by neutrons, releasing heat and more neutrons to sustain the chain reaction. The heat boils water into high-pressure steam, which spins turbines connected to generators to produce electricity. The steam is then cooled and reused.

When It Is Made

The science of nuclear fission was developed in the 1930s, notably through Enrico Fermiâ€™s research. The first controlled chain reaction happened in 1942. In 1951, the Experimental Breeder Reactor in Idaho generated the first electricity from nuclear power. The first community-use nuclear power plant began operating in Obninsk, Russia, in 1954.

Who Made It

Nuclear power development involved many scientists and engineers. Enrico Fermi was pivotal with his work on neutron bombardment. After World War II, the U.S. Atomic Energy Commission played a major role in advancing nuclear energy for civilian use.

Contribution To Society

Nuclear power provides large-scale, low-emission electricity, supporting energy security and climate goals. It has a small land footprint and powers vital services and industries. However, concerns about nuclear waste and accident risks remain key challenges.
