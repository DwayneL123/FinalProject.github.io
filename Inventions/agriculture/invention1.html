Short Description

This resource provides comprehensive information on the importance of irrigation in forage production, detailing various irrigation methods, scheduling techniques, and considerations for efficient water use in forage systems.

Purpose

The primary aim is to educate forage producers about the significance of irrigation in maintaining healthy and productive forage systems, ensuring consistent water supply to meet the needs of forage crops.

How It Is Made

The content is developed by Oregon State University's experts in forage and irrigation management, incorporating research findings, practical experiences, and best practices to create an informative guide for producers.

When It Is Made

The information is continually updated to reflect the latest research, technological advancements, and practical experiences in forage irrigation, ensuring relevance to current agricultural practices.

Who Made It

The resource is produced by Oregon State University's Forage Information System, with contributions from specialists like Dr. David Hannaway, a forage expert at the university.

Contribution To Society

By providing accessible and research-based information, this resource supports sustainable forage production, enhances food security, and promotes efficient water use in agriculture, benefiting both producers and the broader community.
