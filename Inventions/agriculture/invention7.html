Short Description

Green Revolution technologies refer to a set of agricultural innovations introduced in the mid-20th century that dramatically increased food production worldwide, especially in developing countries.

Purpose

The goal was to reduce hunger and boost crop yields by modernizing agriculture with science-based methods, especially in regions facing food shortages.

How It Is Made

These technologies included high-yielding crop varieties (especially wheat and rice), chemical fertilizers, synthetic pesticides, mechanized farm equipment, and expanded irrigation systems. Scientific plant breeding and large-scale research played key roles in their development and spread.

When It Is Made

The Green Revolution began in the 1940s and gained momentum in the 1960s and 1970s, particularly in countries like Mexico, India, and the Philippines.

Who Made It

American agronomist Norman Borlaug is often called the “Father of the Green Revolution” for his work developing high-yield, disease-resistant wheat. International organizations like the Rockefeller Foundation and CGIAR also played crucial roles.

Contribution To Society

Green Revolution technologies helped prevent famines, especially in Asia, and greatly increased global food production. They transformed agriculture into a more intensive and scientific process. However, they also introduced challenges such as environmental degradation, reliance on chemicals, and social inequality in access to resources.
