Short Description

Pesticides are chemical or biological substances used to kill or control pests that harm crops, including insects, weeds, fungi, and rodents.

Purpose

They are used to protect crops from damage, reduce yield losses, and ensure food quality and quantity in agriculture.

How It Is Made

Pesticides are formulated using active ingredients—chemical compounds or biological agents—that target specific pests. These are combined with other substances to improve application and effectiveness. Common types include insecticides, herbicides, and fungicides, often produced through complex chemical synthesis in laboratories.

When It Is Made

The use of natural pesticides dates back thousands of years, but synthetic pesticides became widespread in the 20th century. A major breakthrough came in the 1940s with the introduction of DDT, used extensively during and after World War II.

Who Made It

Swiss chemist Paul Hermann Müller discovered the insecticidal properties of DDT in 1939, for which he won the Nobel Prize in 1948. However, many scientists and companies have contributed to the development of various types of pesticides since then.

Contribution To Society

Pesticides have significantly increased agricultural productivity by controlling pests and reducing crop losses. They have also helped manage disease-carrying insects like mosquitoes. However, their environmental and health impacts—such as pollution, resistance, and effects on non-target species—have led to growing interest in safer and more sustainable pest control methods.
