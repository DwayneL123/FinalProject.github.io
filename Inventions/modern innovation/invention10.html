CRISPR Gene Editing

Genes
DNA strand being cut using CRISPR technology
Preimplantation Genetic Testing

Short Description

CRISPR gene editing is a breakthrough technology that can make precise modifications to an organism's DNA, allowing scientists to effectively adjust gene sequences.

Purpose

It is primarily used to correct genetic diseases, develop disease-resistant crops, promote medical research, and explore potential treatments for diseases such as cancer and genetic diseases.

How It Is Made

CRISPR-Cas9 uses transgenic enzymes to target specific DNA sequences and create precise cuts in the DNA strand, which can remove, modify, or insert genetic material.

When It Is Made

Although genome editing has been a field of research since the discovery of DNA structure in the 1950s, CRISPR-Cas9 was not formally discovered until 2012.

Who Made It

The development of CRISPR-Cas9 was led by Jennifer Doudna and Emmanuelle Charpentier, with assistance from Feng Zhang and several other researchers.

Contribution To Society

Genetics and medicine have greatly benefited from CRISPR gene editing. Genetically modified organisms, animal genetic abnormalities, antibiotic-resistant bacterial treatments, and even HIV removal from infected cells have all been accomplished with it.
