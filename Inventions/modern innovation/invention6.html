Short Description

The Internet of Things (IoT) refers to the network of physical devices embedded with sensors, software, and connectivity that enable them to collect and exchange data over the internet.

Purpose

The purpose of IoT is to enhance automation, improve efficiency, and enable smarter decision-making by connecting devices and allowing them to communicate and respond without human intervention.

How It Is Made

IoT systems are made by integrating sensors and communication technologies (e.g., Wi-Fi, Bluetooth) into everyday objects. These devices transmit data to cloud platforms or centralized systems where it can be analyzed and acted upon.

When It Is Made

The concept began developing in the late 1990s and early 2000s, with the term “Internet of Things” coined by Kevin Ashton in 1999. The technology has rapidly expanded with the growth of internet and mobile devices.

Who Made It

Kevin Ashton is credited with coining the term, but many companies and researchers have contributed to IoT’s development, including tech giants like Cisco, IBM, and Intel.

Contribution To Society

IoT improves everyday life and industrial processes by enabling smart homes, connected vehicles, health monitoring, energy management, and efficient urban infrastructure (smart cities).
