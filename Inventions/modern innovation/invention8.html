Short Description

Virtual reality (VR) is a technology that immerses users in a simulated 3D environment using interactive devices such as headsets, gloves, and motion sensors. It allows users to interact with an artificial world that responds to their actions in real time, creating a lifelike experience.

Purpose

VR enhances gaming, education, medical training, military exercises, and therapy through realistic simulations. Users can practice tasks in a safe, controlled environment, improving learning outcomes and skill development.

How It Is Made

VR is based on advanced computer graphics, motion tracking, and sensory feedback devices that simulate real-life interactions. Components such as stereoscopic displays, force feedback gloves, and spatial audio enhance the sense of immersion.

When It Is Made

The foundations of VR emerged in the 1950s and 1960s as researchers explored interactive computer graphics. The term “virtual reality” was coined by Jaron Lanier in 1987, marking a key moment in its development.

Who Made It

VR was shaped by pioneers such as Jaron Lanier, Morton Heilig, and Ivan Sutherland. U.S. agencies such as NASA and the Defense Advanced Research Projects Agency (DARPA) have played a major role by funding research to further develop VR technology for military and scientific applications.

Contribution To Society

VR has transformed multiple industries by providing immersive experiences that enhance entertainment, education, and healthcare. Surgeons use VR simulations for training, soldiers practice combat scenarios, and therapists help patients overcome anxiety disorders through controlled exposure therapy.
