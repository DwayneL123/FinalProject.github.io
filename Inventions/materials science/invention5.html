Carbon Fiber

Short Description

Carbon fiber is a composite made of thin, strong carbon filaments twisted into yarn. This yarn can be woven into fabric or layered with resin to form rigid structures. Its exceptional strength-to-weight ratio and stiffness make it essential for high-performance uses.

Purpose

Carbon fiber offers lightweight strength and stiffness for critical applications, including aerospace, automotive (for fuel efficiency), sporting goods, construction reinforcement, and other specialized engineering fields where reducing weight without losing strength is vital.

How It Is Made

Carbon fiber production involves chemical and mechanical steps starting with precursor fibers like polyacrylonitrile (PAN), rayon, or pitch. These fibers undergo carbonization—heating at high temperatures without oxygen—to remove non-carbon atoms, leaving strong carbon chains. The fibers are then surface-treated for better bonding with resin and coated with sizing to improve handling. For PAN-based fibers, the process includes spinning, stabilizing by oxidation, carbonizing, surface treatment, and sizing.

When It Is Made

The earliest carbon fibers date back to 1879 when Thomas Edison carbonized cotton or bamboo for light bulb filaments, but these were low-performance. In 1958, fibers with about 20% carbon were developed but had limited strength. True high-strength carbon fiber emerged in 1963 with a new manufacturing process developed at a British research center.

Who Made It

Thomas Edison created an early carbon fiber in 1879 for light bulbs. High-performance carbon fibers were developed near Cleveland, OH, in 1958. The breakthrough manufacturing process unlocking carbon fiber’s full strength happened at a British research center in 1963.

Contribution To Society

Carbon fiber has driven major advances across industries thanks to its unique properties. In aerospace, it enables lighter, more fuel-efficient aircraft. In automotive, it helps create faster, lighter vehicles. Sporting goods like bikes and rackets benefit from its strength and low weight. It’s also used in construction and other fields where high strength-to-weight and stiffness improve performance and efficiency.
