Short Description

A silicon chip, or integrated circuit (IC), is a tiny electronic circuit built on silicon. It forms the foundation of modern electronics, powering computers, smartphones, and many devices by controlling electrical signals at a microscopic level.

Purpose

Silicon chips are the core of electronic systems, integrating millions or billions of components on one chip. They enable fast, efficient processing, storage, and transmission of data for many applications.

How It Is Made

Silicon chips are made through a precise process starting with a pure silicon wafer. Layers of materials are added and patterned using photolithography, where light transfers tiny circuit designs onto the surface. Etching removes excess material, creating transistors and other components connected by conductive paths to form complex circuits.

When It Is Made

Microchip technology was invented independently in 1959 by Jack Kilby of Texas Instruments, who patented miniaturized electronic circuits, and Robert Noyce of Fairchild Semiconductor, who patented the silicon-based integrated circuit.

Who Made It

The pioneers credited with inventing microchip technology are Jack Kilby and Robert Noyce.

Contribution To Society

Silicon chips have revolutionized society, driving the digital age with personal computing, the internet, and mobile communication. They boost efficiency, automation, and connectivity in industries like healthcare, education, and entertainment. Advances in chip technology also improve energy efficiency and support the growing Internet of Things (IoT).
