Short Description

Autonomous vehicles (AVs), also called self-driving cars, are equipped with systems that allow them to perceive their surroundings and make driving decisions without human input. They use a fusion of sensors, cameras, radar, lidar, GPS, and artificial intelligence to navigate safely. These technologies allow the vehicle to detect road conditions, avoid obstacles, and follow traffic laws independently..

Purpose

The primary objective of AVs is to reduce the number of traffic accidents by minimizing human error, which accounts for roughly 90% of road incidents. They also aim to increase road efficiency by reducing congestion and smoothing traffic flow. Additionally, AVs offer vital mobility solutions for the elderly, visually impaired, and individuals unable to drive.

How It Is Made

Autonomous vehicles are developed using a combination of hardware and software:
Sensors & Cameras: Provide real-time data about the vehicle's surroundings.
Radar & Lidar: Detect objects and measure distances.
Artificial Intelligence (AI): Processes sensor data to make driving decisions.
Machine Learning: Allows the vehicle to learn and improve from experience.
Connectivity: Enables communication with other vehicles and infrastructure.

When It Is Made

Development of autonomous vehicles began in the 1980s, with significant advancements in the 2000s. Notable milestones include:
1986: German scientist Ernst Dickmanns developed a self-driving Mercedes van.
2004: Stanford's "Stanley" won the DARPA Grand Challenge.
2009: Google initiated its self-driving car project, leading to the formation of Waymo.
2016: Waymo became a standalone company under Alphabet Inc.

Who Made It

While many have contributed to the development of autonomous vehicles, key figures include:
Ernst Dickmanns: Pioneered early autonomous driving technology in the 1980s.
Sebastian Thrun: Led Stanford's "Stanley" team to victory in the 2005 DARPA Grand Challenge.
Waymo Team: Under the leadership of Sebastian Thrun and Anthony Levandowski, developed Google's self-driving car project.

Contribution To Society

Enhance Safety: By reducing accidents caused by human error.
Improve Traffic Efficiency: Through optimized driving patterns and reduced congestion.
Provide Mobility Solutions: For the elderly and disabled individuals.
Reduce Emissions: By enabling more efficient driving and integration with electric vehicles.
Transform Urban Planning: By reducing the need for parking spaces and enabling new transportation models
